ABOUT RIPE PLANTAIN PORRIDGE.

Indigenous to the south-south/south-east regions of Nigeria, plantain porridge has become a staple food consumed across most of the tribes of Nigeria.

As the name implies, it is a meal made from plantains. With plantain, you can barely get bored as it is quite versatile. From the famous fried plantain(dodo), to pounded plantain, to chips and my all time favorite, plantain porridge, various other cultures within and around Africa make use of plantains in different recipes. It is considered one of the healthiest meals out of Nigeria as it is rich in vitamins and iron. Ripe plantains can either be substituted for or combined with the unripe ones while cooking with certain health considerations or simply for an older person or group.

Seeing that this recipe is about my all time favorite, I recommend ripe plantains simply for it's deliciousness.

Ingredients
S/N Ingredient Measurment
1. Semi-ripe plantains 5 medium sized
2. Palm oil 100ml
3. Fresh tomatoes (sliced) 1 cup
4. Scotch bonnet pepper 2 table spoons
5. Sliced onions 1 cup
6. Seasoning cubes 3
7. Smoked fish 2 medium sized or 1 big sized
8. Ground cray fish Half cup
9. Mackrel fish 500g
10 Ugu(pumpkin) leaves 1 cup
11 Cow skin(optional) 200g
12 Tomatoe Paste 2 small tins
13 Salt 1 teaspoon
Serving: 4 people

Direction:

STEP 1:
Thoroughly clean smoked fish and cow skin with warm water and salt to remove sand. Debone the smoked fish and set aside.
STEP 2:
Put already cleaned cow skin in a pot and season lightly with salt and seaning. Let it cook for 15 minutes then add Mackrel fish. Leave to cook for 8 minutes, debone and set aside. Cut up the cooked cow skin into small pieces and set aside.
STEP 3:
Peel the plantain and cube or cut into desired sizes and shapes.
STEP 4:
Heat up oil in a spacious pot, add sliced onions and fry for 30secs. Add tomatoe paste and allow it fry for 2minutes, pour in sliced tomatoes and scotch bonnet pepper for some heat. Fry for 5 minutes and add the stock from cow skin, add salt and seasoning and bring it to a boil.
STEP 5:
Add plantains and smoked fish. Add water to the level of the plantain, cook for 5 minutes, add deboned fish and cow skin and let it simmer for 2 minutes.
STEP 6:
Add your ugu leaves and let it wilt, stir and taste for salt. Add a little more if necessary and turn off heat.
STEP 7:
Bon appetite!
